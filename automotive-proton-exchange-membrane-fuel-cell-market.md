<p><p>The automotive proton exchange membrane fuel cell market includes various hydrogen storage types: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen involves storing hydrogen at high pressures, offering relatively simple refueling processes. Cryogenic liquid hydrogen stores hydrogen at extremely low temperatures, maximizing energy content and enabling longer ranges. Hydrides, on the other hand, utilize metal hydride compounds to absorb and release hydrogen, providing a safer and more compact storage solution. Each type presents unique advantages suited for specific automotive applications.</p></p>
