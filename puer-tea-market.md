<p><p>The Pu'er tea market comprises two main types: Raw (Sheng) Pu'er and Cooked (Shou) Pu'er. Raw Pu'er is typically aged naturally, undergoing complex flavor development over time, resulting in a lighter and more floral character. In contrast, Cooked Pu'er is fermented and pressed through a process that accelerates aging, resulting in a darker, earthy flavor profile. Both types attract enthusiasts and collectors, with market demand driven by their unique characteristics, aging potential, and cultural significance in tea consumption.</p></p>
