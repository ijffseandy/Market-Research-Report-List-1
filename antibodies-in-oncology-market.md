<p><p>In the oncology market, antibody types play a crucial role in targeted therapies. Murine antibodies are derived from mice, offering specificity but can trigger immune responses in humans. Chimeric and humanized antibodies combine murine and human elements, enhancing compatibility while retaining efficacy. Fully humanized antibodies are entirely derived from human sources, reducing immunogenicity and improving patient tolerance. Other types may include bispecific antibodies and antibody-drug conjugates, providing innovative approaches in cancer treatment by enhancing targeting and therapeutic delivery.</p></p>
