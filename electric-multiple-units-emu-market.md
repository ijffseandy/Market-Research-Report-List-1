<p><p>Electric Multiple Units (EMU) are trains composed of multiple carriages powered by electricity, typically used for passenger transport. In a power centralized market, the train's power supply system is concentrated in one or a few units, providing efficient energy management and maintenance. Conversely, a power decentralized market distributes energy sources across multiple units, enhancing operational flexibility and redundancy. Both market types cater to different operational needs and regional requirements, impacting infrastructure, service efficiency, and overall performance of EMUs.</p></p>
