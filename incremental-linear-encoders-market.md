<p><p>Incremental linear encoders are categorized into three primary types based on their sensing technologies: mechanical, optical, and magnetic. Mechanical encoders use physical contact to detect position changes, offering robustness but limited resolution. Optical encoders utilize light-based sensors, delivering high accuracy and resolution for precise applications. Magnetic encoders rely on magnetic fields for position detection, providing durability and resistance to dust and moisture. Each type serves distinct applications based on performance requirements and environmental conditions.</p></p>
