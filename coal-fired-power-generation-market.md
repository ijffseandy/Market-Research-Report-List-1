<p><p>Coal-fired power generation primarily includes pulverized coal systems and cyclone furnaces. Pulverized coal systems grind coal into fine particles, enhancing combustion efficiency and minimizing emissions. They typically feature large, centralized plants. In contrast, cyclone furnaces utilize a swirling motion to burn coarse coal particles, allowing for more flexibility in materials and reduced capital costs. Both systems play crucial roles in providing electricity, yet they differ in operational methods, fuel types, and combustion techniques, catering to various market needs.</p></p>
