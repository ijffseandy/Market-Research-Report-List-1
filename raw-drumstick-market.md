<p><p>The raw drumstick market is divided into two primary types: bone-in drumsticks and boneless drumsticks. Bone-in drumsticks feature the thigh bone intact, offering flavor and tenderness, making them popular for grilling and roasting. Boneless drumsticks, on the other hand, provide convenience and ease of preparation, appealing to consumers seeking quick meal options. Both types cater to diverse culinary needs, with bone-in favored for traditional dishes and boneless preferred for recipes requiring quick cooking or easy incorporation into various meals.</p></p>
