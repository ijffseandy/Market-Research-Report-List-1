<p><p>Milk allergy clinical trials evaluate various diagnostic methods and treatments through distinct market types. Skin prick tests assess immediate allergic reactions by introducing allergens via the skin. Blood allergen-specific IgE tests measure the immune response in the bloodstream. Oral food challenges involve administering milk to monitor reactions in a controlled setting. Food elimination diets involve removing milk from a patient's diet to identify allergic responses. Each method plays a crucial role in diagnosing and managing milk allergies, driving advancements in clinical research.</p></p>
